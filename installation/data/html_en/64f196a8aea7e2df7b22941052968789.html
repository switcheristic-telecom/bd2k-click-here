Two retired Nuclear Scientists admit to meeting Osama bin Laden : Two retired nuclear scientists who were instrumental in the development of Pakistan's atom bomb have admitted to investigators that they have met Osama bin Laden at least twice this year, it was revealed yesterday. Sultan Bashiruddin Mahmoud and Abdul Majid were held by Pakistani police for questioning for the past two weeks. Both left senior positions at the Pakistan commission about two years ago and established a relief organisation in Afghanistan. But they are known to have had sympathies with the ruling Taliban regime and Mr Mahmoud had in the past advocated the production of surplus weapons-grade plutonium to other countries. .....more     (Copyright: Guardian News Service) (Posted @ 12:00 PST)

Opposition troops enter Kabul: KABUL, Nov 13: Opposition troops entered Kabul today, according to an AFP reporter in Kabul. Fifty or 60 troops of the Northern Alliance, with their distinctive pakool caps, were seen entering the city from the north in jeeps and four-by-four vehicles. They were armed with kalashnikov rifles and rocket-propelled grenade launchers. One group was seen escorting four Taliban fighters. Small crowds of Kabul residents lined the streets in the north of the city to cheer the arrival of the Northern Alliance forces. They chanted "Allah o Akhbar (God is Great)" as the opposition troops chanted back the same.    (AFP) (Posted @ 08:00 PST, Tuesday)

US plane bombs Kabul, triggers blaze: KABUL, Nov 13: A US warplane dropped at least two bombs on Kabul in the early hours of today morning, sparking a large fire in the southeast of the city, residents said. The plane flew over Kabul at 1:30 am (2100 GMT Monday) and two loud explosions were heard. Minutes later an AFP reporter saw a fire burning in the southeast of Kabul.    (AFP) (Posted @ 06:00 PST, Tuesday)

Indian attack on Siachen glacier repulsed: ISLAMABAD, Nov 12: Government today said it had repulsed an Indian infantry and artillery attack on a border post in Kashmir, inflicting "heavy casualties". The Pakistan military public relations unit said a "company of the Indian army comprising more than 60 soldiers supported by heavy artillery and mortar fire" launched the attack on Sunday night. It said the bodies of three Indian soldiers remained near the Pakistani post in Gyong sector of the far northern Siachen glacier. An army spokesman in New Delhi denied there were any Indian casualties and said India did not launch the attack.    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

Airliner with more than 250 on board crashes in New York: NEW YORK, Nov 12: An American Airlines Airbus A-300 packed with 246 passengers and nine crew slammed into a residential area of New York City today and exploded, moments after taking off from John F. Kennedy International Airport. With New York still in deep trauma two months after terror attacks which killed 5,000 people, American Airlines Flight 587 bound for Santo Domingo, capital of the Dominican Republic, crashed into the Rockaway area in the borough of Queens.    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

UN sealed off after airliner crash in New York: UNITED NATIONS, Nov 12: New York City authorities sealed off the United Nations headquarters, where world leaders were meeting today when a commercial airliner crashed shortly after takeoff from John F. Kennedy International Airport. "No vehicles and no pedestrians are being permitted to enter the UN complex," the chief of UN security, Mike McCann, told staff over the internal public address system after the American Airlines Airbus A300 came down.    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

Taliban confirm opposition breakthrough around Kabul: ISLAMABAD, Nov 12: Taliban confirmed today that opposition forces had breached their front lines around Kabul, but said the advance had been halted and they retained control of the capital. "It is true that the opposition have broken through our front lines," Taliban ambassador to Pakistan Abdul Salam Zaeef was quoted as saying by the Pakistan-based Afghan Islamic Press (AIP).    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

UN must move quickly on Afghanistan: Annan- UNITED NATIONS, Nov 12: The United Nations must move quickly to help set up a representative government in Afghanistan, UN Secretary General Kofi Annan said today as opposition forces closed in on Kabul. Speaking to reporters after top-level talks with countries most involved in the Afghan crisis, Annan said his special envoy, Lakhdar Brahimi, would "step up his work consulting the Afghan parties."    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

Opposition launches drive toward Kabul: KABUL, Nov 12: Afghan opposition forces backed by tanks and artillery broke through Taliban lines north of Kabul today, routing the Islamic militia and leaving themselves a clear path to the capital, a spokesman said. But the drive stopped short of Kabul as the opposition appeared to be respecting for the moment US appeals that they stay out of the capital while talks continue on formation of a broad-based administration. "The Taliban are evacuating their positions. We are almost completely in control of the Shomali plain (just north of Kabul)," opposition Northern Alliance spokesman Waisudin Salik told AFP.    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

Afghan opposition captures Kunduz: Iran news agency - TEHRAN, Nov 12: Kunduz has been captured, a member of the Northern Alliance opposition movement told the state IRNA news agency today. The spokesman of Abdul Rashid Dostam, a key opposition commander whose forces were among those that captured the strategically important city of Mazar-i Sharif, was quoted as saying his forces had also "killed and captured a great number of Taliban forces. "Dostam's forces were able to enter (and capture) the town of Kunduz early on Monday after six hours of clashes," said the spokesman, who was identified only as General Shafi.    (AFP) (Posted @ 13:55 PST)

US B-52s carpet bomb Taliban positions north of Kabul: RABAT, Nov 12: US B-52 planes carried out carpet bombing on Taliban positions north of Kabul today as opposition forces were preparing to go on the offensive.   (APP/AFP) (Posted @ 12:05 PST)

Opposition to launch offensive on Kabul: KABUL, Nov 12: The Afghan opposition, buoyed by successes in northern Afghanistan are geared to launch their long-awaited offensive toward Kabul today but will not enter the capital, a commander said. "We have decided to advance to the doorstep of Kabul but not enter inside the capital. We will launch our offensive within the next half an hour or hour," General Afzal Aman told AFP at 0545 GMT. "Our forces are all ready and prepared to launch their offensive and they are in their positions," the Northern Alliance commander said. "Our strategy is not to enter Kabul but we do want to advance to the gates of Kabul," Aman said. "We want to reach Kotal Khair Khanna," a hilltop area on the edge of the Shomali planes just northwest of the capital.    (AFP) (Posted @ 11:05 PST)

Taliban free Pakistani journalist: PESHAWAR, Nov 12: Pakistani journalist Mukkaram Khan was freed today after a month in Taliban detention in Afghanistan and has returned to Pakistan, family members told AFP.    (AFP) (Posted @ 23:00 PST)

Three Canadain warships pass Suez Canal to join war: CAIRO, Nov 12 : Three Canadian warships passed through Egypt's Suez Canal to the Red Sea today to take part in U.S.-led military operations against Afghanistan, a Canadian diplomatic source said. The frigates HMCS Charlottetown and Iroquois and supply ship HMCS Preserver will arrive in the Gulf region later this week. "They are heading to the Arabian Sea as part of the international coalition against terrorism and will join American and British ships," the Cairo-based diplomat told Reuters. Another frigate, HMCS Vancouver is also heading to join the U.S.-British forces via the Pacific Ocean, he said.    (Reuters) (Posted @ 16:45 PST)

US urges Afghan opposition to surround, not occupy, Kabul: WASHINGTON, Nov 11: The US administration today urged Afghan opposition forces to surround but not occupy Kabul, though the Northern Alliance did not rule out moving on to the capital after it captured a pivotal northern city. "We think it would be better if they were to 'invest' the city, make it untenable for the Taliban to continue to occupy Kabul," Secretary of State Colin Powell said on NBC. Defense Secretary Donald Rumsfeld said opposition fighters should seek to isolate the capital, get the ruling Taliban to surrender and make way for a broad-based government.    (AFP) (Posted @ 00:15 PST)

Lone accuses India of assassination bid: NEW DELHI, Nov 12: Abdul Gani Lone, a prominent member of the All Party Hurriyat Conference, an umbrella forum of Kashmir separatists, said a November 1 attack on his home was an assassination bid either by New Delhi or the state administration of Indian Kashmir.
