    Fence Notes:
    Martinsville safety worker update: Randy Hall of Spencer, VA. is still in critical condition at Baptist Hospital in Winston-Salem, NC as of this afternoon. His condition has not been upgraded since he arrived at the hospital on Sunday. For those of you who may not know, Randy was working with the track clean-up crew and during a caution that began with lap 291 he fell off the clean-up truck. He suffered a 4-6 inch cut on the back of his head. The emergency room staff of the Memorial Hospital of Martinsville & Henry County, which works our Infield Care Center, stabilized him and AirCare took Randy to Baptist Hospital. 
    [ Martinsville Speedway PR ]

    Scott Hansen, driver of the No. 52 Oakwood Homes Chevrolet, will make his first career start in the NASCAR Craftsman Truck Series at Louisville Motor Speedway this weekend Hansen has competed once on the .438-mile track in the ASA series in 1994. In that event, he started 12th and finished seventh.

    FAIN Motorsports: Due to NASCAR® restrictions on rookie drivers that requires them to make their debut on tracks of 1/2 mile length or less, veteran driver Mark Day will be driving for FAIN Motorsports on Oct. 31st in the Sams Town 250 instead of Chris Douglas. Chris Douglas will remain a key figure in the team’s NASCAR Busch Series effort by handling all the marketing and sponsor relation duties for the event. Sponsorship for the #13 FMS Pontiac will be provided by Buck & Bass Sports Centre, CATCHFENCE.com, and Custom Van & 4x4 Center.[ Press Release ]

    Jeff Gordon wins without Evernham as he wins his 6th race of the season, and his 48th of his career. Gordon took the checkered flag 0.198 seconds ahead of Dale Earnhardt in the NAPA AutoCare 500 at Martinsville, Virginia. Geoffrey Bodine came in third, followed by the Wallace brothers Rusty and Kenny rounding out the top 5 finishers. [ Unofficial Results ]
